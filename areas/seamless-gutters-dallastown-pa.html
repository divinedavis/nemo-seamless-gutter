Why Dallastown homes need good gutters

Much of Dallastown's housing is older — twin homes and detached homes built when the borough was a tight-knit cigar-making town — and many sit on noticeably sloped ground. On a hillside lot, water that overshoots a gutter or pours from a poorly placed downspout doesn't just sit; it runs, finding the low side of the foundation and any path toward a basement. Good drainage planning matters more here than on flat ground.

Add Pennsylvania's seasonal swings on top of that grade. Heavy spring storms send a lot of water down those slopes at once, fall leaves from the borough's older trees clog runs quickly, and winter's freeze-thaw cycle pries at any gutter holding standing water. Older homes with long, low-pitched runs are especially prone to overflow if the system isn't set up right.

Pitched and routed for the grade

We set each run with the correct slope and place downspouts to carry water downhill and well clear of your foundation, so a hard rain drains instead of pooling against the house. Where tree cover is heavy, we'll talk through whether guards are worth it for your property.

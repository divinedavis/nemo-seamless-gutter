Why Dover homes need good gutters

Dover's housing is a real mix. The newer developments around town tend to have simple, steep roofs that dump a lot of water fast in a downpour, so downspouts need to be sized and placed to keep up. The older farmhouses scattered through the township are a different challenge — long fascia runs, porches, dormers and decades of settling that can leave gutters out of pitch and prone to overflow.

Sitting in open, rolling country, many Dover properties take the full force of Pennsylvania's spring thunderstorms with little to slow the rain down. Add the leaves that blow in from surrounding tree lines each fall and the freeze-thaw swings that work on any gutter holding standing water through winter, and you have a recipe for fascia rot and foundation runoff if the system isn't doing its job.

Sized for new builds and old farmhouses

We match the gutter and downspout capacity to how your particular roof sheds water, and we'll flag spots where an older home's drainage was never quite right to begin with. The result is gutters that carry water cleanly away from the foundation through every season.

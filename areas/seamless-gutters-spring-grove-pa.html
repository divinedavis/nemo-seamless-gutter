Why Spring Grove homes need good gutters

Spring Grove is the most rural and wooded of the towns we serve, with many homes set on larger lots ringed by mature trees, and the recreation land around Lake Marburg and Codorus State Park keeping the whole area green. All that canopy is beautiful — and it's relentless on gutters. A roof surrounded by oaks, maples and pines collects leaves, needles and seed pods through much of the year, and a clogged gutter on a remote property can go unnoticed until it's already overflowing.

Pennsylvania's weather only adds to the load. Heavy spring and summer storms dump a lot of water on open, rolling terrain, fall brings a steady rain of leaves from every direction, and winter freeze-thaw cycles crack and loosen any gutter holding standing water. On a wooded lot that you'd rather not be climbing a ladder to clean every few weeks, a gutter system that's properly sized — and often guarded — makes a real difference.

Built for heavy tree cover

We size gutters and downspouts for the real volume your roof sheds and talk honestly about whether leaf protection earns its keep on your particular lot. For many tree-shaded Spring Grove properties, gutter guards mean far fewer cleanings and far less worry about overflow.

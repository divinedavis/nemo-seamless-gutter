Why Hanover homes need good gutters

Hanover is one of the older boroughs in York County, and a lot of its housing stock shows it — sturdy brick rowhomes and detached homes with steep roofs and long fascia runs that were never designed around modern downspout placement. Those long runs need gutters with proper pitch, or water pools and spills over onto brick and mortar that doesn't like staying wet.

The borough's mature, leafy streets are a big factor too. Tall maples and oaks shade much of town, and every fall they drop more than enough debris to pack a gutter solid. Pair that with Pennsylvania's heavy spring rains and the winter freeze-thaw cycles that pry at clogged, overflowing gutters, and a neglected system can quickly start damaging fascia, soffits and basement walls.

Built for Hanover's weather and trees

We size gutters and downspouts to handle the volume of water a Hanover roof actually sheds in a hard storm, and we'll talk through whether leaf protection makes sense for your specific tree cover. The goal is a system that keeps working through leaf season and ice season without constant attention.

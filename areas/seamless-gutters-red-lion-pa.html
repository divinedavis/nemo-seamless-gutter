Why Red Lion homes need good gutters

Red Lion grew up around its early industry, and much of the borough's housing dates back generations — twin homes, porch-front homes and detached older houses packed onto modest in-town lots. When homes sit this close together, an overflowing or misaligned gutter doesn't just threaten your own foundation; it can send water straight onto a shared wall or a neighbor's yard.

The borough's mature street trees are part of what makes Red Lion pleasant to walk, but they shed a steady supply of leaves and seed pods onto every roof below. Combine that fall debris with Pennsylvania's heavy spring rains and the winter freeze-thaw cycles that lift and crack gutters holding standing water, and tight in-town lots leave very little room for error.

A precise fit for tight borough lots

Because we form each gutter run on-site to your exact roofline, downspouts can be placed where they'll carry water cleanly to the street or away from the structure, even on narrow lots. We'll also talk through leaf protection where the tree cover warrants it, so you're not back up a ladder every few weeks.

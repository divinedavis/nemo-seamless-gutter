Your gutters only protect your home when water can actually move through them and away from the house. When leaves pack in or a section starts pulling loose, water backs up and overflows. We clean gutters out and repair what's keeping them from draining the way they should.

Gutter cleaning

A thorough cleaning keeps water flowing and gives us a chance to spot small problems before they grow. Here's what that includes:

  • Remove leaves and debris — clear out the buildup of leaves, needles, twigs, and grit that collects in the trough.
  • Flush the downspouts — run water through to break up and clear any clogs so the system drains freely.
  • Check the flow — confirm water moves toward the outlets and drains away from the foundation rather than pooling or overflowing.
  • Bag and haul off the debris — we clean up after ourselves and take the mess with us.

Gutter repair

If your gutters are still in good shape overall but a few things have gone wrong, repairs are often the practical fix. Common repairs we handle:

  • Re-secure sagging gutters — reattach and support runs that have started to droop.
  • Re-pitch for proper flow — adjust the slope so water runs to the downspouts instead of sitting in the gutter.
  • Reseal leaking seams and end caps — stop drips at joints and corners.
  • Replace damaged sections — swap out lengths that are dented, split, or rusted through.
  • Reattach or replace downspouts — fix loose, crushed, or missing downspouts so water gets carried away.
  • Fix gutters pulling away from the fascia — refasten gutters that have separated from the house.

Signs you need service

Not sure whether it's time? These are the things to watch for:

  • Water spilling over the front edge during rain
  • Gutters sagging or pulling away from the house
  • Peeling or streaked fascia boards behind the gutters
  • Water pooling near the foundation
  • Plants or grass growing in the gutter
  • It's been over a year since the gutters were last cleaned

When to clean your gutters

For most homes, twice a year — once in the spring and once in the fall — keeps things flowing. If you have a lot of trees close to the house, you'll likely want them cleared more often, since leaves and needles fill the gutters faster.

When repair isn't enough

Sometimes gutters are simply worn out, rusted, or too far gone to keep patching. If that's where yours are, we can quote seamless gutter replacement so you're not paying to repair the same problems again and again.

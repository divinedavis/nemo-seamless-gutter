What are half-round gutters?

Half-round gutters get their name from their shape: a rounded, U-shaped trough that looks like a pipe cut in half lengthwise. That's a contrast to the more common K-style gutter, which has a flat back and an angular, decorative front face designed to mimic crown molding.

The half-round profile is the traditional, classic look. You'll see it most often on older, historic and architecturally detailed homes, where the smooth curved line fits the period far better than a modern angular gutter. If you're restoring or maintaining an older home — or simply want that timeless appearance — half-round is usually the right call.

Materials we offer

We install half-round gutters in two materials, and the right one depends on your home and your budget:

  • Aluminum half-round — a cost-effective choice that's lighter and available in a wide range of colors, so you can match or complement your trim while keeping the classic rounded shape.
  • Copper half-round — a premium option that naturally ages to a distinctive patina over time and is known for being very long-lasting. Copper is a beautiful, high-end look, but it does cost more than aluminum.

We're happy to walk you through both during your estimate so you can weigh appearance, longevity and cost for yourself.

Why choose half-round

Homeowners pick half-round gutters for a few good reasons:

  • Period-correct curb appeal — the rounded profile suits older and historic homes and preserves their original character.
  • Smooth interior — with no inside corners, the curved trough is easy to rinse out and tends to resist buildup compared with more angular shapes.
  • Durable — built with quality material and proper hangers, half-round gutters stand up well to weather over the long term.
  • Pairs with round downspouts — the look is completed with matching round downspouts for a cohesive, finished appearance.

Things to know

Half-round gutters are a great fit for the right home, but there are a couple of practical points worth understanding up front:

  • Cost — half-round typically costs more than standard K-style because it uses more material and takes more labor to install. We'll lay out exactly what your project involves in a free written quote, so you know what to expect before you commit.
  • How they hang — half-round gutters are usually supported with brackets or hangers made specifically for the round profile, rather than the hidden hangers used on K-style runs.
